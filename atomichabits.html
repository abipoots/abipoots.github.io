Atomic Habits - James Clear
A habit is a behaviour that has been repeated enough times to become automatic.
Why do they exist? - to solve the problems of life with as little energy and effort as possible.

LAW 1: MAKE IT OBVIOUS


THE HABITS SCORECARD



Create a list of all your daily habits. Label them good(+), bad (-) or neutral (=).
Examples:
  • wake up = check my phone -
  • turn off alarm = brush my teeth +

POINTING AND CALLING


Say out loud the action you are thinking of taking, and what the outcome will be.

IMPLEMENTATION INTENTIONS


I will [BEHAVIOUR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION] *specificity is key*



HABIT STACKING

e.g. After I pour my morning cup of coffee, I will meditate for sixty seconds.
e.g. After I meditate for sixty seconds, I will write my to-do list for the day.
e.g. After I write my to-do list for the day, I will immediately begin my first task.

FINDING THE RIGHT TRIGGERS FOR YOUR HABIT STACKS


Make a list of daily habits and a list of things that happen to you each day. (e.g. sun rises, sun sets, song ends, text message received)

Search for the best place to layer your new habit into your lifestyle.

DESIGN YOUR ENVIRONMENT

BE THE ARCHITECT


It is easier to associate a new habit with a new context than to build a new habit in the face of competing cues.
Habits are associated not with a single trigger but with the entire context (e.g. drinking more in social situations because hearing music, seeing beers on tap, watching friends order drinks)
Make cues a big part of your environment:
  • Want to drink more water? Place water bottles around the house.
  • Want to practise guitar more often? Place your guitar stand in the middle of the living room.


LAW 2: MAKE IT ATTRACTIVE



IT IS THE ANTICIPATION OF A REWARD - NOT THE FULFILLMENT OF IT- THAT GETS US TO TAKE ACTION.
DESIRE IS THE ENGINE THAT DRIVES BEHAVIOUR.
EVERY ACTION IS TAKEN BECAUSE OF THE ANTICIPATION THAT PRECEDES IT.

THE DOPAMINE SPIKE

TEMPTATION BUNDLING

(Pairing actions you WANT to do with actions you NEED to do)


  1. After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [HABIT I NEED].
  2. After [HABIT I NEED], I will [HABIT I WANT].

e.g. If you want to watch sports, but you need to make more sales calls.
  1. After I get back from lunch, I will call 3 potential clients. [need]
  2. After i call 3 potential clients, I will check ESPN. [want]

JOIN A CULTURE WHERE YOUR DESIRED BEHAVIOUR IS THE NORMAL BEHAVIOUR

THE SEDUCTIVE PULL OF SOCIAL NORMS: WHATEVER HABITS ARE NORMAL IN YOUR CULTURE ARE AMONG THE MOST ATTRACTIVE BEHAVIOURS YOU'LL FIND.
We tend to imitate the habits of the close, the many and the powerful.
